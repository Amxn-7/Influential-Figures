Freedom Fighters of India

Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh was a prominent revolutionary leader who played a crucial role in India's independence struggle. Known for his boldness and sacrifice, he became a symbol of courage and youthful rebellion against British rule.

Rani Lakshmibai

One of the leading figures of the Indian Rebellion of 1857, Rani Lakshmibai, also known as the Queen of Jhansi, was a fearless warrior who fought valiantly against British forces, becoming a symbol of resistance and bravery.

Subhas Chandra Bose

Subhas Chandra Bose was a leader of the Indian nationalist movement who formed the Indian National Army (INA) to fight against British colonialism. His famous slogan "Give me blood, and I will give you freedom" inspired many to join the struggle for independence.

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Sardar Patel, also known as the "Iron Man of India," played a key role in the integration of princely states into the Indian Union after independence. His contributions to India's unity and independence are widely respected.

Mangal Pandey

Mangal Pandey was an Indian soldier whose actions sparked the Indian Rebellion of 1857. He is remembered as one of the first martyrs who rebelled against British rule, marking the beginning of India's independence movement.
